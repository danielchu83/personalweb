A small museum of AI product failure modes.

I collect the moments where intelligent systems behave differently from what the demo promised, then try to understand what product design can do about it.

001

Evidence failure

The agent said it used a tool. It didn't.

What happened
The response cited a command-shaped source without reliable execution evidence.
What surprised me
The answer sounded more grounded than it was.
Open question
How should products expose proof of work without overwhelming the user?
Status
Observed repeatedly.
002

Tooling failure

The more powerful tool made the system worse.

What happened
A generalized tool expanded the agent's routing problem.
What surprised me
Capability improved while predictability got worse.
Open question
Where should product constraints live: prompt, tool, or interface?
Status
Still studying.
003

Evaluation failure

The eval passed, but the workflow failed.

What happened
The test checked the chat answer, not the real artifact or side effect.
What surprised me
The grader and the product lived in different worlds.
Open question
How do you grade work that happens outside the transcript?
Status
Actionable pattern.
004

Recovery failure

The AI wrote data without a recovery path.

What happened
A write became durable state before the system made repair obvious.
What surprised me
Undo felt like UX, not infrastructure.
Open question
What should every AI write path return as a receipt?
Status
Design principle.
005

Interface failure

The interface hid too much control.

What happened
The product made the agent feel simple by hiding the user's real levers.
What surprised me
Less visible complexity created more supervisory work.
Open question
What should the user control, and what should the system infer?
Status
Open exhibit.
