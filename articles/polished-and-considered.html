Taste / craft

The difference between polished and considered

A short essay on why good work feels inevitable only after many small choices.

A piece of work can look polished and still feel thin. The edges are smooth, the copy is clean, the mockup is tidy, but it does not show much evidence that anyone wrestled with the actual use case.

Considered work has a different texture. You can see the choices made, the alternatives rejected, and the details that survived contact with real use.

I am less interested in polish as a surface treatment and more interested in the kind of care that leaves useful evidence behind.
