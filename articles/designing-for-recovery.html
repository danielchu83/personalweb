Designing for recovery before designing for scale

Notes on logs, retries, dashboards, and the boring tools that make ambitious work less fragile.

A system can look ready because the happy path works. Then the first confusing failure arrives: a job half-runs, a retry might duplicate work, and the dashboard says something happened without showing enough detail to know what to do next.

I care about recovery before scale because scale usually makes those failures louder. Before a system gets more users, it should help the team see what happened, decide what is safe to retry, and recover without guessing.

The recovery question

Before asking whether a system can handle more users, ask whether it can help you understand and recover from the first confusing failure.

Useful signals

A good recovery path usually starts with readable logs, a small number of meaningful dashboards, and enough context to tell whether a retry is safe or just noise.

My current read is that these tools are not cleanup work after the product is done. They are part of what makes the product usable under stress.
