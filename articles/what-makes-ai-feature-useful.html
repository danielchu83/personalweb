What makes an AI feature feel useful?

A working list: speed, control, reversibility, clear failure modes, and enough context to avoid making the user babysit it.

A useful AI feature should leave the user with less work, not a new supervision job. If I ask it to summarize a thread, update a record, or draft a reply, I should not have to inspect every hidden step just to feel safe.

My current list is simple: it should be fast, reversible, clear about failure, and honest about what it does not know.

The feature does not need to feel magical. It needs to fit into the workflow well enough that the user can trust it, correct it, and move on.
