When an assistant should ask, act, or stay quiet

A product note about agency, trust, and workflows that feel helpful instead of noisy.

An assistant can be annoying even when it is technically helpful. It drafts the message before the user has decided what to say, files the ticket before the owner is clear, or asks a question after the next step was already obvious.

The hard part is deciding when action helps and when it creates more work for the user. I think about it as three modes: ask, act, or stay quiet.

Ask

Ask when the decision changes the goal, the cost, the audience, or the user’s trust boundary.

Act

Act when the next step is reversible, clearly implied, and saves the user from repetitive coordination.

Stay quiet

Stay quiet when the system has nothing useful to add, when the user is still forming intent, or when a suggestion would mostly create noise. Restraint is part of the interface.
