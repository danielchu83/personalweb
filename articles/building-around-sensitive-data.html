Building around sensitive data

How to write honestly about Atlas while keeping private source code, personal data, and operational details out of public view.

This is a placeholder article for the Atlas writing lane. The goal is to preserve the shape of the learning without publishing the raw materials that made the learning possible.

What can be public

Architecture diagrams, design tradeoffs, debugging methods, synthetic examples, and decision records can usually tell the story without exposing the real records, keys, logs, or source code.

What should stay private

Personal data, raw inputs, credentials, internal identifiers, private repositories, and screenshots containing real user content should be replaced with careful summaries or representative examples.
