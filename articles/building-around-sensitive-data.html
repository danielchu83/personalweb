Atlas / technical essay

Building around sensitive data

How to write honestly about Atlas while keeping private source code, personal data, and operational details out of public view.

Atlas gives me useful writing material because the problems are concrete: private data, local tools, failures, recovery paths, and agent behavior. It also creates a boundary problem. The parts that make the story real are often the parts that should not be public.

The rule I use is simple: publish the lesson, not the private material. The public version should keep the problem clear without exposing source code, records, keys, logs, or personal data.

What can be public

Architecture diagrams, design tradeoffs, debugging methods, synthetic examples, and decision records can usually tell the story without exposing the real records, keys, logs, or source code.

What should stay private

Personal data, raw inputs, credentials, internal identifiers, private repositories, and screenshots containing real user content should be replaced with careful summaries or representative examples.

If the public version only works because it reveals private context, I treat that as a sign that the example needs to be smaller or more synthetic.
