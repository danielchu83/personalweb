AI Products Need Taste, Not Just Intelligence

How defaults, constraints, interaction design, and product judgment shape whether AI becomes useful.

Two AI products can use a similar model and feel completely different. One asks at the right moment, shows what changed, and gives the user a way back. The other interrupts too often, hides the risk, and leaves the user cleaning up after it.

That difference is product taste. It shows up in defaults, constraints, empty states, confirmations, and recovery paths. The model may be capable, but those small choices decide whether the product feels useful or annoying.

I would want to judge an AI product less by how smart it sounds and more by how carefully it handles the moments where the user has to trust it.
