What I noticed when I stopped rushing the first draft

A practical note about slowness, clarity, and making better decisions sooner.

When I rush a first draft, I usually produce a convincing version of the wrong thing. The sentences move, the outline fills in, and it can feel like progress until I realize I have not found the actual question yet.

Slowing down at the start helps me notice what kind of piece I am writing. Is this an example I need to understand? A tradeoff I am trying to name? A story that needs less argument and more scene?

The slower start often saves time later because there is less cleanup around a false shape.
